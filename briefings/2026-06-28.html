OpenAI previews GPT-5.6 (Sol, Terra, Luna) — but you can't have it yet

The headline event of the week slipped past the last two briefings, so we catch it up: OpenAI previewed the GPT-5.6 family — flagship Sol, mid-tier Terra, and low-cost Luna — with published API pricing, but limited the rollout to a small set of trusted partners at the US government's request over the models' stronger cyber and bio capabilities. Pricing and positioning are public, so you can plan against them even though you can't call them yet. On the regulatory front, APRA and ASIC have written to regulated entities demanding a step-change in AI-risk management. Quiet on new dev-tool releases in the Jun 27–28 window.

OpenAI's new generation is three models: Sol, the flagship; Terra, a balanced everyday tier; and Luna, the fastest/cheapest. Even without access, the pricing reshapes your cost model: Terra at $2.50/$15 undercuts the prior flagship tier while claiming parity — that's the number to benchmark your per-request economics against when GA lands, and Luna at $1/$6 is a credible cheap-classifier/extraction tier. Don't rewrite anything yet (preview ids aren't production-safe), but pencil Terra into your next cost projection and plan an eval harness so you can swap-and-measure on day one of GA.

Your Radar

Availability and regulatory signals to watch.

GPT-5.6 access is gated — limited to "trusted partners" at US government requestOpenAI · Jun 26

OpenAI restricted the GPT-5.6 preview to a small group of vetted partner organisations, citing a US government request tied to the models' uplift in cyber and biology capability. There is no public waitlist or application, and individual developers are not eligible. OpenAI framed the limits as exceptional rather than the new norm, with broader availability still planned.

Treat GPT-5.6 as announced-but-unavailable: you can design against its published pricing and capabilities, but you cannot build on it today, and there's no queue to join. If a roadmap item assumes a GPT-5.6-class model, keep a current-gen fallback (GPT-5.5 / Claude / Gemini) as the shipping path and revisit when GA actually opens — gated previews have slipped before.

APRA & ASIC tell regulated entities to lift AI-risk managementCFR · Jun 23

The Council of Financial Regulators' quarterly statement notes that APRA and ASIC have written to their regulated entities calling for a "step-change" in the management of cyber and AI-related risks, observing that frontier AI models have intensified cyber threats for financial institutions. It reinforces the standing line that existing AFSL obligations — efficient, honest, fair — apply fully to AI-assisted processes.

If you sell into Australian advice or wealthtech, your buyers' boards are now being asked to evidence AI governance, not just claim it. That's a sales-and-product signal: model documentation, validation records, audit logs and human-in-the-loop controls become procurement checkboxes. Building those into the product now turns a compliance burden into a differentiator when an advice firm's risk committee reviews your tool.
