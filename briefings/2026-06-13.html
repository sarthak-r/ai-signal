Anthropic pulls Fable 5 & Mythos 5 on a US export order

A light news day dominated by one event: a federal export-control directive forced Anthropic to disable its two newest models for everyone, hours after the last briefing shipped. One genuine open-weights release alongside it. No padding — two things happened.

Fable 5 & Mythos 5 disabled — globallyAnthropic · Jun 12

At 5:21pm ET on Jun 12 a US government export-control directive ordered Anthropic to block all foreign-national access to Fable 5 and Mythos 5, citing a national-security concern. Rather than partially comply (which would have meant blocking its own foreign-born staff and a swath of users), Anthropic disabled both models for every customer worldwide. All other models — Opus 4.8, Sonnet 4.6, Haiku 4.5 — are unaffected.

If you pinned claude-fable-5 in the API, or set it as your Claude Code default after the Jun 9 switch, those calls fail now — revert to Opus 4.8 today. Anthropic says it's "working to restore access as soon as possible" and will share technical detail within 24 hours, but gave no restoration date. Treat the timeline as open.

A jailbreak finding triggered a full commercial recallStrategy signal · Jun 12

The directive operationalises the Jun 2 executive order's frontier-model review regime. The cited concern was a narrow, non-universal jailbreak — per Anthropic, "asking the model to read a specific codebase and fix any software flaws." Anthropic complied but publicly disagrees, warning that if this standard generalised it "would essentially halt all new model deployments for all frontier model providers."

Model availability and release cadence are now a regulatory variable, not just an engineering one — a model you depend on can vanish in an afternoon with no notice. Don't wire hard single-model dependencies: keep a fallback model wired and your model id in config, so a recall is a one-line change rather than an outage.
