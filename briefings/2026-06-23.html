Fable 5 leaves Claude subscription plans — now bills at API rates, double Opus 4.8Anthropic · Jun 23

From 9 June, Fable 5 was included at no extra cost in Pro, Max, Team and seat-based Enterprise plans. That window closed today: Fable 5 is removed from included plan limits, and once you exhaust your standard plan usage, further Fable 5 calls draw on prepaid usage credits billed at API rates — $10 / 1M input, $50 / 1M output (cached reads $1/1M), exactly 2× Opus 4.8's $5/$25. Credits are topped up under Settings → Usage with a $2,000/day redemption cap; when they run out, access reverts to plan limits with no loss of service. Anthropic frames this as a capacity decision, not a permanent tier, and says it intends to fold Fable 5 back into subscriptions "once capacity allows" — no date given.

If anything in your stack quietly defaulted to Fable 5 during the free fortnight — an agent loop, a batch job, a "use the best model" toggle — it's now metered overage, not bundled allowance, and a runaway loop can drain prepaid credit at twice the Opus rate. Audit where Fable 5 is actually wired in today, pin Opus 4.8 (or Sonnet 4.6) explicitly anywhere you need predictable per-token cost, and set a credit ceiling before re-enabling Fable on any unattended path. For most B2B wealthtech workloads the Fable→Opus quality gap rarely justifies the 2× spend on production inference.
