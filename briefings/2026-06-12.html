The Catch-Up — February to June 2026

The opening brief. Everything from the last four months that still matters if you're building AI products today — filtered to one test: can you use it, does it change your costs, or does it change your roadmap? Daily briefs from here on are 7–10 bullets; this one is the back-fill.

The five arcs of the period

  1. Long context became table stakes. 1M-token windows are now standard across Claude, GPT-5.4/5.5, Gemini, Grok 4.3, DeepSeek V4 and MiniMax M3 — the RAG-vs-long-context math has changed.
  2. Agents moved into the platform. Every major lab now sells hosted sandboxes, harnesses and subagent primitives — build-vs-buy for agent infra is a different question than it was in January.
  3. Open weights caught the frontier in coding. GLM-5.1 and MiniMax M3 took #1 on SWE-bench Pro past closed models, while Meta left the open field — China and NVIDIA filled it.
  4. The bill changed shape. Usage-based metering arrived everywhere (Copilot credits, Agent SDK credit pools, per-minute sandboxes), alongside the densest deprecation calendar yet.
  5. Australian regulators showed their hand. TPB, APRA, ASIC and AUSTRAC all moved on AI in the same window — governance features are becoming procurement requirements.
