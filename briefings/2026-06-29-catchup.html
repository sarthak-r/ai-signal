The week the builder's job became loop design — and the loop's blast radius became the problem

"I don't prompt Claude anymore. My job is to write loops." Boris Cherny's line crystallized the week's loudest debate — and the rest of the catch-up reads as its consequences. The app layer raced to ship the controls a loop needs: durable approvals, session revert, per-host egress, mid-run steering, phone-based supervision. And a parallel current of voices and research warned what loops break — runaway multi-agent spend, eroded human comprehension, prompt injection reframed as unfixable "role confusion," and skill catalogs where 36% carry injection.
