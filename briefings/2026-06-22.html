Codex Record & Replay turns a screen recording into a reusable skillOpenAI · Jun 18

OpenAI's Codex macOS app (v26.616, CLI 0.141.0) added Record & Replay: demonstrate a repetitive desktop workflow once and Codex drafts an editable SKILL.md — when to use it, required inputs, steps, verification — that you replay later with new values (a date, a file). Built on Computer Use; macOS only; initial rollout excludes the EEA, UK and Switzerland.

This is the cheapest path yet to automate the GUI-bound chores that resist scripting — filing an expense, pulling a recurring report, configuring an issue. For a small team, recording a workflow into a versioned SKILL.md beats writing brittle automation. Note the regional gate and that the recording captures whatever's on screen — keep client PII out of frame.

Your Radar

Cost controls and a compliance flag for an Australian wealthtech build.

Fable 5's restricted-jurisdiction list is still unpublished — don't put it on a critical AU path yetAnthropic · ongoing

The nationality-based controls that gated Fable 5's return verify billing identity, not geography at inference time, and Anthropic has not published the list of restricted jurisdictions. No enterprise can complete a defensible compliance review against an unpublished list.

For a B2B wealthtech product under AFSL obligations to act "efficiently, honestly and fairly," an LLM whose availability rules you can't document is hard to sit in a regulated workflow (e.g. SoA drafting). Until the jurisdiction list is public, keep Fable 5 to internal/experimental use and run client-facing inference on a model with stable, documented access — Opus 4.8 or Sonnet 4.6.
