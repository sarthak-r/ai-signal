Build Patterns

Two ships and a Product Hunt through-line, all circling the same move: put the human where they steer best — the review, the approval, the surface they already use.

Plannotator: annotate the diff before it's a PR — and the notes go straight into the agent's next turnShow HN · 6 Jul

A local diff viewer for git, jj and Perforce that shows committed, uncommitted and untracked changes together, so you can review the whole change while it's still on your machine. You select ranges and leave comments, concerns or suggested edits; hit send and everything exports as Markdown anchored to exact files and lines, straight into your agent session (Claude, Codex, OpenCode). The core viewer is manual; the AI layer is optional. MIT / Apache-2.0, free, fully local.

This is a clean answer to "how do I steer an agent mid-build without typing paragraphs into a chat box." The interaction unit becomes a line-anchored annotation — the same gesture as a human code review — and it feeds the loop before a PR exists, closing the tail where agents drift. Two things to steal even if you don't adopt it: review-before-PR as a stage in your own agent flow, and Markdown-anchored-to-lines as the format for handing structured human feedback back to a model. Reviewing the agent's work is becoming the primary human interface; this is what that interface looks like.

CodeMote: your CLI agent's approval prompts, on your phone — live terminals and encrypted notificationsProduct Hunt · 6 Jul

An iPhone remote for CLI coding agents (143 upvotes): live terminal views, approval notifications, and encrypted connections, so you can watch and approve or deny an agent's risky steps from your pocket while it runs on your machine.

The gate-the-action instinct this desk flagged on 4 Jul, made ambient: the human veto no longer requires sitting at the terminal. The pattern worth naming is asynchronous approval — the agent runs long, pauses at the risky step, and pings a human who's elsewhere. For any agent that touches money, records or client data, "it waited for a phone tap before doing the dangerous thing" is a control you can describe to a compliance officer. Build the pause-and-notify boundary into your agent; the delivery channel (phone, Slack, email) is a detail.
